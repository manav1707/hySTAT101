Flip-clock options

All three are ticking once per second. Watch them on this device for a few seconds — pick the one that feels smoothest. Each box shows a fake countdown (D / H / M / S).

A. Split-flap (current)

Card splits horizontally. Top half rotates down/away, bottom half rotates up to reveal new digit. Iconic "airport board" look — most realistic, most moving parts.

Best for: premium / nostalgic feel. Drawback: most likely to stutter on slower devices.

B. Full-card flip

The whole digit rotates 180° around its horizontal axis. At the midpoint, the digit swaps. One smooth motion — no split, no two-stage timing.

Best for: balance of motion + simplicity. Smoother than A on most devices.

C. Digit slide

Old digit slides up and out, new digit slides up into place. No 3D rotation at all — just translateY. Cheapest to render.

Best for: minimal, ticker-tape feel. Smoothest of the three; least "physical."
